Audi Daily Log App

What is it?

A Power Apps application designed and developed to digitize and optimize daily information logging across three work shifts, tailored to specific user needs. A total of 11 SharePoint lists served as the database, paired with an intuitive, user-friendly interface.

What was its added value?
  • PDF Report Generation: Generates a consolidated PDF report from all data entries, that can be sended to each member of the team via email with a single button press.
  • Centralized Daily Logging: Provides an intuitive interface to record operations across all three shifts, improving clarity and consistency of daily logs.
  • Production Goal Tracker: Automatically calculates production targets and line stoppages, eliminating manual table creation.
  • Photo Logging for Failures: Allows users to capture and store images directly into the system for real-time visualization of issues.
  • Excel Macro Integration: Custom Excel macros enable fast data uploads by copying and pasting large tables directly into the database, boosting efficiency.
  • Multi-Platform Accessibility: Information can be accessed and edited both through Power Apps and Excel, ensuring flexibility and continuous data syncing.

'CINDY BJ2' - Bluetooth Controlled Mobile Vehicle

What is it?

CINDY BJ2 is a cylindrical Bluetooth-controlled vehicle developed as a university mechatronics project. The goal was to integrate mechanical design, embedded programming, and mobile app control into a single functional prototype. The vehicle responded to Bluetooth commands sent from an iOS app and was built entirely from scratch, challenging the team to solve problems in design, stability, power management, and system communication.

How It Came to Life?
  • Optimized Mechanical Design (SolidWorks): Designed a compact cylindrical chassis that efficiently distributed weight and provided structural integrity. Carefully positioned internal components (ESP32, H-bridge, batteries, breadboard) to avoid rotation and maintain balance during movement.
  • Embedded Control via ESP32: Programmed the microcontroller using the Arduino IDE to interpret Bluetooth signals and convert them into precise PWM control for motor movement. Integrated the L298N H-bridge for coordinated bidirectional control of dual DC motors.
  • Team Integration with iOS App: Collaborated with teammates developing the iOS control app in Xcode using Swift, ensuring smooth Bluetooth communication between the phone and the ESP32. This allowed full remote control of the vehicle through a clean mobile interface.
  • Collaborative Engineering Process: The project was a true team effort, combining design, programming, and problem-solving. It represented our desire to go beyond the classroom and challenge ourselves to build something unique, functional, and exciting.
